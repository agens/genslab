Meet The Faithful Brothers.

Is there a Northern Soul scene in Tel Aviv? Whether or not you’ve ever asked yourself this question, you might find the answer surprising – yes there is. If you're a soulie and in town, look for Tel Aviv Soul Club, and you might find yourself swaying across a talcum-powder covered floor to Tel Aviv's own unique blend of classic Northern Soul 45s and early 1960's R&B sounds.

Chances are that those 45s will come out of the record boxes of one of the brothers Neeman, Johnnie and Bin, the sons of an Israeli diplomat, who travelled abroad extensively as children, in the 1960's and 1970's, discovering along the way the wonderful world of rare soul music, while their peers were either into progressive rock or disco.

Tel Aviv Soul Club, aka TASC, is the brainchild of Yashiv Cohen, who, ten years ago, lured the brothers Neeman, whose sets have been hitherto confined to their respective living rooms, into playing their records to the Tel Aviv public.

Yashiv is also the lead singer of Men of North Country (MONC), a Tel Aviv band with a distinct sound, blending soul, rock, and British pop, that has recorded two albums for the London based label Acid Jazz.

The story, as Yashiv tells it, is that since Neeman means Faithful in Hebrew, he woke up one morning with the crystallization that TASC must create a spin-off, a soul band, called the Faithful Brothers, where the original Neeman/Faithful brothers would be joined by top Tel Aviv musicians to create new original soul music and R&B inspired records. With a nod to the Northern Soul slogan Keep the Faith, the name seemed too perfect to waste. The fact that the Faithful Brothers were not musicians at the time did not bother a man of vision like Yashiv. One day, he sent Johnnie some lyrics he wrote, asking him to compose music for it. Well, Johnnie called Bin, and they met in Johnnie's record room. With Johnnie playing some of the few chords he knew on his dusty old acoustic guitar, out came Teenage Frost, the first ever musical composition of the Neeman Brothers, recorded by MONC.

It took a few more years of feet dragging, but gradually the brothers succumbed to their fate, to continue their musical progression, from collectors to DJs to musicians. Johnnie took some guitar lessons from a couple of Tel Aviv guitar stalwarts, Bin took to the piano, and while at it, the brothers began pouring out some of the influences they caught over the years, into new songs.

It is finally all coming together, as The Faithful Brothers – now an eight piece band behind Yashiv, complete with a mighty brass section – have recorded a few of the brothers Neeman's songs, and have had their first live appearance, opening for and backing American soul artist Eli Paperboy Reed on a visit to Tel Aviv.

“This is unbelievable”, says Johnnie. ”After decades of revering all those great soul records, to actually be able to compose new music and play it, with some fabulous musicians, and to see the inside of a recording studio for the first time, now when I'm in my 50s, is just mind blowing”.

The first four songs recorded by the Faithful Brothers are:

Crossroads of Love – an R&B tinged tune written by Johnnie

Downtown, Uptown – a R&B duet featuring Tel Aviv soul singer Carmi Shimron, written by Johnnie and Bin to Yashiv's lyrics.

Night Man – a Northern Soul influenced instrumental composed by Bin.

One More Time – a soul ballad written by Johnnie, Yashiv, and the band.

Personnel:

Yashiv Cohen – lead vocals

Johnnie Faithful – guitar

Bin Faithful – piano

Eviatar Oren – bass, guitar, 12 string guitar

Omri Behr – Hammond and piano

Yuval Guttman, Rani Birenbaum – drums

Sefi Sizzling – trumpet

Ongy Sizzling – tenor Sax

Roey Bar Yehuda – baritone Sax

Carmi Shimron – vocals on Downtown, Uptown

Elb Digger Story

"...There is too much good music out there that we can't play on our vinyl nights!" Driven by the love of diggin’ vinyl and their devotion to ska, soul & punk music, 3 guys from Hamburg joined forces and connected with their musician friends all over the globe to promote their music. "A lot of the smaller bands nowadays go their way without a label and have often times not the possibility to press vinyl" The digital world offers plenty of ways to get heard but at the same time it generates an enormous overload, that a lot of great musical nuggets simply get lost in the flood.

Elb Digger's goal is not to press everything they can get their hands on but to dig deeper for real floor filling dance treasures that get washed up on the shores of river Elbe in Hamburg St. Pauli. We met so many good musicians that had nothing to sell on their shows and just simply said: for more music go to www... blah blah blah. A missed opportunity and there is nothing you can carry home from your new musical discovery. The web helps to promote but cannot substitute a physical medium like a 7" Single or even a whole Album on Vinyl. Dig it.
